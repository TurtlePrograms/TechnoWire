1791-1871

Charles Babbage

Known as the 'father of the computer' due to his development of the mechanical analytical engine, an early concept of a programmable computer.

1815-1852

Ada Lovelace

The first programmer in history, thanks to her work on Babbage's analytical engine and her notes that laid the foundation for modern programming languages.

1910-1989

William Shockley

One of the pioneers of the semiconductor industry and co-inventor of the transistor, which laid the foundation for modern electronics.

1929-present

Gordon Moore

Co-founder of Intel Corporation and formulated the famous "Moore's Law," predicting the regular doubling of computer power.

Steve Wozniak (1950-present) and Steve Jobs (1955-2011)

Steve Wozniak, Steve Jobs

Co-founders of Apple Inc. and responsible for the development of the first successful personal computer, the Apple I and the Apple II.

Larry Page (1973-present) and Sergey Brin (1973-present)

Larry Page

Sergey Brin

Co-founders of Google, the world's most widely used search engine, and innovators in the field of search algorithms and internet services.

1969-present

Linus Torvalds

Creator of the Linux operating system, one of the most influential open-source projects in the history of computer science.

1984-present

Mark Zuckerberg

Co-founder of Facebook, a social media platform that has changed the way people worldwide communicate and share information.

1955-present

Bill Gates

Co-founder of Microsoft and one of the world's wealthiest individuals, with significant influence on the personal computer industry.

Chad Hurley (1977-present), Steve Chen (1978-present), and Jawed Karim (1979-present)

Chad Hurley

Steve Chen

Jawed Karim

Co-founders of YouTube, which revolutionized the way videos are shared and viewed online.

1963-present

Kevin Mitnick

A former hacker and later a security expert who significantly contributed to the understanding and improvement of cybersecurity.

1967-present

Rich Skrenta

Known for his contributions to the development of the first computer virus, "Elk Cloner," which he wrote when he was still a teenager and is considered a historic moment in computer science.
