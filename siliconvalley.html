History of programming languages

Welcome to the History of Programming Languages page.





1956

William Shockley

received the Nobel Prize in Physics for his contributions to the invention of the transistor. William Shockley referred to the group of 8 employees who left him as the "Traitorous Eight. Moore's Law is an observation made by Gordon Moore, co-founder of Intel, stating that the number of transistors on a microchip roughly doubles every two years, leading to exponential growth in computing power.

1975

Beginning of Silicon Valley(HP in Garage)

Silicon Valley emerged approximately 84 years ago in 1939. Silicon Valley is located in the state of California, United States, and covers an area of approximately 3,000 square kilometers(1158,31 square miles). The tree that has become the symbol of Silicon Valley is the "sequoia" or "redwood" tree. Silicon Valley gets its name from the fact that silicon (an important material in the semiconductor industry) and valley (due to its geographical location) come together.

1975

The Altair 8800 is released

The Altair 8800 was a popular microcomputer kit released in 1975 and is often considered one of the first personal computers. It played a significant role in the early history of personal computing

1977

Apple II is presented

Approximately 200 Apple I computers were made, and their value varies, but rare specimens can be worth hundreds of thousands of dollars. Daniel Kottke creates a portable Apple II in a suitcase The founders of Apple are Steve Jobs, Steve Wozniak, and Ronald Wayne. The hippie culture influenced the development of the PC because many tech pioneers, such as Steve Jobs, were influenced by the counterculture of the 1960s and 70s, leading to creative and innovative approaches in technology.

1984

Launch of the Macintosh

What made the Macintosh special was its graphical user interface (GUI) and the mouse, which were revolutionary for personal computers at the time.

1992

Launch of Windows 3.1

1995

Launch of Windows 95

Windows 95 was launched with the song "Start Me Up" by The Rolling Stones.

1998

Google is founded

AltaVista was a search engine company that was popular in the early days of the internet. Google was founded by Larry Page and Sergey Brin The first Google server was built using LEGO blocks.
