1956

William Shockley

Gordon Moore, a co-founder of Intel, made significant contributions to the invention of the transistor, which ultimately earned him the Nobel Prize in Physics. He's also known for coining the term "Moore's Law," which observes that the number of transistors on a microchip tends to double roughly every two years. This exponential growth in transistor density has been a driving force behind the rapid increase in computing power. Additionally, William Shockley gained notoriety for referring to a group of eight employees who left his team as the "Traitorous Eight," a term that has become part of tech industry lore.

1975

Beginning of Silicon Valley(HP in Garage)

Silicon Valley, which emerged approximately 84 years ago in 1939, is situated in the state of California, United States, encompassing an expansive area spanning approximately 3,000 square kilometers (1,158.31 square miles). The emblematic tree that represents Silicon Valley is the towering "sequoia" or "redwood" tree. The valley owes its name to the convergence of "silicon," a vital material in the semiconductor industry, and "valley," a reference to its geographical location. This unique combination of factors has made Silicon Valley a globally renowned hub for technology and innovation.

1975

The Altair 8800 is released

TThe Altair 8800, a widely acclaimed microcomputer kit, made its debut in 1975 and is frequently regarded as one of the pioneering personal computers. This groundbreaking device played a pivotal role in shaping the early history of personal computing.

1977

Apple II is presented

Around 200 Apple I computers were manufactured, with their worth varying considerably. Some rare specimens can command prices reaching hundreds of thousands of dollars. Notably, Daniel Kottke engineered a portable version of the Apple II housed in a suitcase. The founding trio of Apple consisted of Steve Jobs, Steve Wozniak, and Ronald Wayne. Their collaboration marked the genesis of a tech empire that would shape the future of computing. The development of personal computing was significantly influenced by the hippie culture of the 1960s and 70s. Many tech pioneers, including Steve Jobs, were deeply impacted by the counterculture movement of that era. This influence led to the adoption of creative and innovative approaches in technology, contributing to the unique and transformative character of the personal computer industry.

1984

Launch of the Macintosh

The Macintosh computer stood out for its revolutionary features, notably the introduction of a graphical user interface (GUI) and the integration of a mouse. These innovations were groundbreaking for personal computers, setting the Macintosh apart and marking a significant milestone in the evolution of computing technology.

1992

Launch of Windows 3.1

Windows 3.1 brought significant improvements to the Windows operating system, including the introduction of the TrueType font system. This allowed Windows applications to use scalable fonts like Arial, Courier New, and Times New Roman in various styles and sizes without relying on third-party technology.

1995

Launch of Windows 95

Windows 95 was launched with the song "Start Me Up" by The Rolling Stones.

1998

Google is founded

AltaVista was a prominent search engine company during the nascent stages of the internet. However, it eventually faded into obscurity with the rise of more dominant search engines like Google. Speaking of Google, it was founded by the visionary duo of Larry Page and Sergey Brin. Notably, the very first Google server was ingeniously crafted using LEGO blocks, reflecting the company's early commitment to innovative solutions and creative problem-solving in the world of technology.

1999

WIFI is introduced

Wi-Fi, a technology that enables wireless internet access using radio signals, was introduced in 1999. It allows devices to receive data from the internet via wireless transmitters and exchange information, revolutionizing how we connect to the internet.

2004

Facebook is founded

Facebook was founded by Mark Zuckerberg and his college roommates. This social networking platform quickly became a global phenomenon, connecting people online and playing a pivotal role in the evolution of social media.

2005

YouTube is founded

Today On average, about 500 hours of video are uploaded to YouTube per minute, and approximately 350 million photos are uploaded per day.

2006

Twitter is founded

offering a platform for users to share short, 140-character messages known as tweets. Twitter's real-time, concise format has had a significant impact on communication, news dissemination, and online culture.

2007

iPhone is presented

a revolutionary device that combined a phone, an iPod, and internet connectivity in a single package. The iPhone's introduction marked a significant moment in the evolution of mobile technology, leading to the smartphone era and influencing countless industries.

2008

Tesla Roadster Introduced

Tesla Motors, founded by Elon Musk and others, introduces the Tesla Roadster, an all-electric sports car, marking a significant step in the development of electric vehicles.

2010

Instagram Launch

Instagram, a popular photo and video-sharing social networking service, is launched by Kevin Systrom and Mike Krieger.

2011

Introduction of Siri

Siri, the virtual assistant developed by Apple, is introduced with the release of the iPhone 4S.

2012

SpaceX's First ISS Mission

SpaceX, led by Elon Musk, becomes the first privately funded company to send a spacecraft to the International Space Station, marking a historic moment in space exploration.

2014

Google's Self-Driving Car Project

Google unveils its self-driving car project (Waymo), signaling a major advancement in autonomous vehicle technology.

2016

Pokémon Go

Pokémon Go, an augmented reality mobile game developed by Niantic, becomes a global sensation, showcasing the potential of augmented reality in the gaming industry.

2017

Waymo's Autonomous Vehicle Testing

Waymo, a subsidiary of Alphabet Inc. (Google's parent company), begins testing fully autonomous vehicles on public roads in Arizona, pushing the boundaries of self-driving technology.

2019

Uber's IPO

The IPO of ride-sharing giant Uber takes place, making it one of the most significant tech IPOs in recent years.

2020

Impact of COVID-19 on Tech

The COVID-19 pandemic accelerates the adoption of remote work and digital communication tools, transforming the way people work and interact, with Silicon Valley playing a crucial role in these changes.

2021

SpaceX's All-Civilian Mission

Elon Musk's SpaceX conducts the first all-civilian mission to space, further advancing the commercial space industry and space tourism.
